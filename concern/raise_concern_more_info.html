What happens when I raise a concern?

There are two ways of raising a concern. First, you can raise a concern directly. Your details will be sent straight to your chosen contact, who will follow up with you to discuss the problem. They may decide to conduct an informal investigation into the issue.

Second, you can enter the details of your concern into our matching system. We'll hold them securely, unless somebody else comes forward to identify a concern with the same individual. If that happens, we'll send both of your concerns on to your points of contact, so that they can be discussed or investigated simultaneously. We'll also email you to let you know that that's happened.

In both cases, we'll put the details you've given us into a pdf document, and send it in an email to the person you've asked us to contact. Anybody else you've identified will not be notified. However, the person we contact - whether that's your line manager or somebody else - may choose to pass the details of your concern onto anybody else you've named, as part of their investigation process.
