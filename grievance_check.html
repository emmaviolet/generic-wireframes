Before you start

Before you file a formal grievance, you're encouraged to try to resolve the issue with the individual concerned. There are a number of services available within the Ministry of Justice to help you do this, detailed here. A formal grievance is the appropriate next step if attempts have already been made to resolve the issues informally, or if you feel that an informal resolution would not be possible or appropriate.

In order to file a grievance, we'll need a few details from you.

  • Your name
  • The name of the individual concerned
  • Details of what happened
  • The date of the incident or incidents
  • Details of any next steps you would like to see

Once completed, you'll have a chance to review your report before it's submitted. We'll then send your grievance to your line manager, unless you'd prefer an alternative point of contact.
